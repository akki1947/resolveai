How to Complain Against Amazon India – Complete Guide 2026

Updated: March 2026 | Based on latest consumer protection laws and Amazon policies

If you've received a defective product, a refund delay, or poor service from Amazon India, you have multiple options to seek resolution. This guide covers every step from contacting Amazon customer care to filing a consumer court case. [citation:1]

⚖️ Legal Note: Under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, you have the right to seek redressal for defective products, refund delays, and unfair trade practices.

📞 Option 1: Contact Amazon India Customer Support

Always start here – most complaints are resolved at this level. [citation:1]

Amazon India Contact Details

📱 Phone: 1800-1200-1571 (Toll-free)
✉️ Email: cs-reply@amazon.in
💬 Chat: Amazon app/website → Help → Contact Us
📮 Postal: 26/1, Brigade World Trade Centre, 10th Floor, Dr. Raj Kumar Road, Malleshwaram (W), Bangalore – 560055 [citation:1]

Pro Tip: Always ask for a Complaint Reference Number / Ticket ID. Without this, you cannot escalate. [citation:6]

🐦 Option 2: Social Media Escalation

Companies respond faster on public platforms. [citation:1]

1
Tweet at @AmazonHelp

Include your issue, order ID, and tag @AmazonHelp. Use hashtags like #AmazonRefund #ConsumerComplaint.

2
Post on their Facebook page

Facebook.com/AmazonIN – public posts often get faster responses.

⚖️ Option 3: Escalate to Grievance Officer

If customer care doesn't resolve in 7 days, escalate to Amazon's Grievance Officer (mandated by law). [citation:5]

Grievance Officer Contact

Email: grievance-officer@amazon.in

Response time: Within 48 hours (legally required)

🏛️ Option 4: National Consumer Helpline (NCH)

Free mediation service by the government. They contact Amazon on your behalf. [citation:5]

📞 Helpline: 1915 (toll-free)
🌐 Portal: consumerhelpline.gov.in

⚖️ Option 5: Consumer Court (E-Daakhil)

If all else fails, file a case online without a lawyer. [citation:6]

E-Daakhil Portal

Website: edaakhil.nic.in

Filing fee: ₹0 for claims up to ₹5 lakhs, nominal fee above.

Time limit: File within 2 years of the issue.
