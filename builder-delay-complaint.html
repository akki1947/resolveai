Builder Delay Complaint RERA India – Complete Guide 2026

Updated: March 2026 | Based on RERA Act 2016 and state RERA regulations

If your builder has delayed possession beyond the promised date, you have strong legal rights under RERA. This guide explains your options, the complaint process, and how to claim compensation.

⚖️ Your Rights Under RERA Section 18

Two Primary Remedies:

1
Continue with project and claim interest

You can stay in the project and the builder must pay interest for every month of delay until possession. The interest rate is typically SBI MCLR + 2% (approx 10-11% currently).

2
Withdraw and get full refund with interest

You can exit the project and claim a full refund of the entire amount paid, along with interest. The builder must refund within the statutory timeline.

📋 Step 1: Verify Your Possession Date

Check two documents:

  • The Agreement for Sale – has the promised possession date.
  • The MahaRERA / UP RERA portal – the date declared by the builder during registration must match.

If the builder has taken an extension, check if it was legitimately approved by RERA. Unjustified extensions can be challenged.

📄 Step 2: Send a Formal Notice to the Builder

Before filing a complaint, send a legal notice under Section 35 of the Consumer Protection Act. This shows you attempted resolution.

Notice Should Include:

  • Project and unit details
  • Promised possession date
  • Current delay status
  • Demand for interest or refund (specify which)
  • 7-15 days deadline to respond

🏛️ Step 3: File Complaint with RERA

RERA complaints are filed online with the state RERA authority.

State Portal Key Details
Maharashtra (MahaRERA) maharera.mahaonline.gov.in Most active; complaints resolved in 60 days typically
Uttar Pradesh (UP RERA) up-rera.in Covers Noida, Greater Noida, Lucknow
Andhra Pradesh (AP RERA) rera.ap.gov.in Detailed online filing system

Documents Required:

  • Agreement for Sale copy
  • All payment receipts
  • Possession letter / timeline commitments
  • Correspondence with builder
  • RERA registration number of project

Filing fee: Varies by state – typically ₹5,000-₹10,000 for individual complaints.

⏱️ Step 4: What Happens After Filing

1
Admission

RERA admits your complaint and issues notice to the builder.

2
Hearings

Both sides present evidence. RERA aims to resolve within 60 days.

3
Order

RERA can order interest payment, refund, or compensation. Orders are enforceable as arrears of land revenue.

⚖️ Alternative: Consumer Court (E-Daakhil)

You can also file in consumer court, especially if seeking higher compensation.

E-Daakhil Portal: edaakhil.nic.in

No lawyer needed for claims up to ₹50 lakh.
