🔄 The Airtel Complaint Loop – And How to Escape It

Many users report a frustrating cycle: complaint lodged → technical team calls but never visits → complaint auto-closed. [citation:2]

1
Get the technician's number

When they call, save their number and confirm the visit time. If they don't show, call them back immediately.

2
If no visit, re-open ticket

Call customer care, give your ticket number, and report that the technician never visited. Demand escalation.

3
Escalate to Nodal Officer

If unresolved after 2-3 attempts, ask for the Nodal Officer's email. Format: nodal.officer@in.airtel.com or search for your circle's specific email.

⚖️ Tier 2: Appellate Authority (TRAI Mandated)

Under Telecom Rules, you have the right to appeal if your complaint is unsatisfactorily closed. [citation:6]

Appeal Format

Send an email to the Appellate Authority with subject: "Appeal against closure of Complaint Docket No: [YOUR NUMBER]"

Request a Personal Hearing (video or in-person). This is your legal right under the Telecom Consumers Complaint Redressal Regulations, 2012. [citation:6]

🏛️ Tier 3: Department of Telecommunications (DoT) / CPGRAMS

If Airtel's internal process fails, approach the government. [citation:6]

🌐 Portal: cpgrams.dot.gov.in
📞 Helpline: 1800-111-222 (DoT)

Strategy: File on CPGRAMS under "Dept of Telecommunications". They have 21 days to resolve. [citation:6]
